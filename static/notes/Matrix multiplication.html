Matrix multiplcation

Understanding matrix multiplication as a function is simple, but developing the intuitive sense of how it is the combination of two transformations is more difficult.

The difficulty comes from the context shift. After a transofrmation X and Y (or \( \hat{i} \) and \( \hat{j} \) ) the pane (or span) is now wherever \( \hat{i} \) and \( \hat{j} \) land.

Matrix composition makes this simple.

\( \dfrac{1}{\sqrt{2}} \begin{bmatrix}1 & 1 \\ 1 & -1\end{bmatrix} \left(\dfrac{1}{\sqrt{2}} \begin{bmatrix}1 & 1 \\ 1 & -1\end{bmatrix}\right) = \dfrac{1}{2} \begin{bmatrix}2 & 0 \\ 0 & 2\end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix}1 & 0\\0 & 1\end{bmatrix} \)
