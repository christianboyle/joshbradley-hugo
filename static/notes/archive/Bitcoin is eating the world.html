When Mark Andreesen wrote the article "Software is eating the world" he was sitting on the cusp of the digital revolution. He envisioned a world where the internet would create more opportunity and innovation than previously possible.

20 years later, we are still riding that wave of technological innovation. All participating in a propserous, fruitful economy that makes people's lives easier.

Now we are upon yet another, greater revolution, the revolution of money. An era of unprecendented innovation and social mobility.

And Bitcoin is the technology that is making all of this possible.

Bitcoin isn't a secret to any of us. Many of us even understand how it works.

The real mystery is why are people so excited about it? Why are billionaires putting 90% of their wealth into something that doesn't physically exist?

The answer lies in understanding currency and how Bitcoin perfectly satisfies its properties.

The five core properties of currency are:

  • Durability
  • Divisibility
  • Recognizability
  • Portability
  • Scarcity

History shows us that every trade society naturally selects a currency that best fits these properties. By large the free market selected gold as the ideal currency because of how it fulfills these properties.

Gold never degrades, can be divided into dust, is relatively easy to recognize and verify, is portable, and is very scarce.

This is why governments and financial institutions have used it as their primary store of wealth for thousands of years.

However, gold is not perfect. It is heavy, conterfeiters can debase it with other material, and the supply grows by 2% every year.

Bitcoin on the other hand, is perfectly portable, infinitely divisible, cyptographically secure, and has a dimishing and ultimately fixed supply. It is the perfect currency.

If you're thinking, "Okay, so Bitcion is marginally better than gold, why is that revolutionary?" you're exactly right.

The primary issue that Bitcoin seeks to solve is not issues with gold. Bitcoin is trying to replace fiat currency.

In 1971 when Nixon abolished the gold standard, USD lost an essential property of currency. It was no longer scarce.

The Federal Reserve was granted full authority to create USD as it saw fit, and distrubute it to those who it thinks are most deserving.

This immediately created an artificial monetary system that disproportionally rewarded holders of assets and debt, and diminished holders of currency (primarily the middle class).
