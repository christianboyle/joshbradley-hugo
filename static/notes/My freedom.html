I am at the pinnacle of choice. I have never been more free, more happy, or more motivated to work towards a future which I would be proud of.

The danger is that freedom is so easy to throw away.

Every job offer gives me second thought, stability, security, a guaranteed career track. Those things sound damn attractive.

But they come at the cost of freedom.

And it's difficult to see, because it isn't a full sacrifice, I would still have my time and money to be free with.

It's a loss of my free mind.

No longer would it seem interesting to dig into the US federal budget, to take apart a crypto company, or to simply build an app because it's fun.

My energy and my life would be focused on that narrow career track. Software engineer. One day a CTO...

But what about me?

I am not a single dimensional being. I, Joshua Bradley, am composed of an entirely unique experience that I would necessarily surrender to a career.

Why not turn myself into a career?

My vast curioisity and abiility to learn have always guided me to undestanding our world deeply. From spiritual, interpersonal relationships to the fundamental mathematics powering the world's most important algorithms.

I am fully aware that my undstanding of all of these topics is softmorish, but that doesn't invalidate that which I do know.

And that is no excuse to throw away my diversity.

I offer something that people want. That is evident from nearly everyone I speak to. But I have never been able to define it beacuse it's not a single quality of attribute. It's me.

I need to preseve and nurture that for as long as possible.

If I find myself on hard times, without money or hope, then I can return to the narrow dimension of a career and build myself back up from there.

But I am brimming now, don't pour the magic of life into somebody else's cup. Wait until you need a refill.
