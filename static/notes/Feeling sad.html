Feeling sad

I know myself well enough to know that I get sad. I am tempted to say depression, but that is a grade of sadness that I don't want to put myself in.

It comes in clusters. Life events lead to a low that often feels amplified by consecutive events. Often family swarms in, because of timing or with good intentions, but it's exhausting.

They want to fix things and see their son happy. I want to sleep.

It's a balance of creating the space I need and putting in enough time with them that they don't feel alienated. The same goes for my friends.

I know I should do better with the things I eat, watch, do, and when I sleep. Small changes help. I need to do better.

I want to be motivated and excited by the things I'm doing. I want to feel like every action is full of purpose. I want to direct my intent to things that matter.

But then I get sad. I think about Rebecca, and my other failed relationships. I think of friends lost. I think of my lonliness.

The world can feel so distant. The social distancing doesn't help.

I don't know what the purpose of writing this is. Part of me wants to rant, the other wants to help me. Another just wants something to do that I feel motivated to do.

Why not all three?

A rant

It's always a loss of attraction. Most breakups I've had are centered around attraction. Obviously, attraction extends beyond just physical attraction, but that is still the reason. A loss of excitement, a loss of intimacy, a feeling that I make a great friend but maybe not a great boyfriend.

Fuck.

Make up your goddamn mind. Don't tell me, "Wow, I like that you're so calm, and patient, and nice..." to turn around and be bored. To want somebody that wants to go party, hunt, whatever the fuck it is. I just want to be with you. You knew before we dated that I don't enjoy people or most social settings. They're stressful and unrewarding. I love thinking and developing myself, but you don't see that. You see a boring nerd on his computer and I hate it.

I want to feel connected without feeling like I need to be something.

Some help

Eat better, watch less Youtube, sleep better hours, and spend some time outside or exercising. You can do this. I know it hurts, but you need to find space to heal, it is the only way forward.

Visualize the woman you want. Someone nice, easy-going, down to earth, intelligent, and kind. Someone who you enjoy laughing with and sleeping with and living with. Someone who you trust completely who you know would make a good mother.

Were your past relationships her? No.

Move forward and be the man that will meet and attract her. Don't devolve into a sad, miserable, angry man who spends his time on little things. Don't give up.

What motivates me

Right now, it is a career change. Sometimes challenges in your projects arise and it becomes easier to turn on a video and turn off your brain. But it is directly counterproductive.

Fix in your mind the future you want.

An entrepreneur with fuck you money who made a company that made a difference in the world. You get there by working your ass off. Not just on work, but on yourself, your mindset, your focus. Get there.

Sadness is a disease

Saddness is a disease. It corrupts your memories with misery, anger, and anguish.

The constant question of "Why?" gnaws at your mind. Why did she leave? Why did it go wrong? Why does this keep happening?

I long for the days when I felt hopeful. The happy, naive young man who was going to change the world is gone. Only his forgotten shell remains.
