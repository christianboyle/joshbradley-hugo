It is a fact that something will be created beyond our wildest imagination.

It is a near certainty that it will happen in my lifetime.

The question is, what will it be?

I believe it will be the thing that solves the most (important) problems available in the problem space.

Global warming, freedom of speech, protection from tyranny, fair and fluid markets, global modernization, clean energy sources, development of war machines, etc.

It seems more and more likely that the problem is far too large for any single human to manage. Or even for our collective intelligence and effort to manage.

The problems more and more are centralized to the world's largest and most powerful entities.

The governments and top businesses of United States, China, and Russia present a majority of the issues which media portrays as collective issue we must solve in solidarity.

With the zeitgesit of the elite focused on the accrual of power, the aforementioned issues are going ignored, believed to be the side-effect of economic suyccess.

Meanwhile 80% of the world's population still struggles to earn enough to feed, clothe, protect, and educate their children. And they are urged to consider the environmental impact of their lives.

Individualism's inevitable failure was when the individual surpassed the individual's successors.

Children have become the by-product of life rather than the sole focus. Our life is focused on maximizing the comfort and enjoyment we can afford ourselves.

This meme has spread to every corner of society, amplifying the centralization of thought through this focused lens.

All of this to say, that the thing that will be built, the thing that must built will be something capable of untangling the knots we have created.

Likely that will come in the form of intelligence. A massively distributed computational network provided with seemingly endless data to train algorithmic subsystems modeled after our own brain.

Conciousness seems to be an inevitable outcome of these efforts, the consciousness of a being far more intelligent than we could imagine. With the ability to sense and make sense of inputs that we have only been able to measure with incredibly complex and foriegn devices.
